What you may be experiencing

You have probably tried every anti-dandruff shampoo on the shelf.

The flakes settle for a week or two, then return. The scalp may feel oily and irritated at the same time, itchy by the afternoon, and visibly flaky on dark clothing. In Singapore's humidity, it can feel like a battle that resets every single day.

Persistent flaking is often dismissed as a hygiene issue. It is not. Washing more aggressively frequently makes it worse.

A scalp that flakes persistently is a scalp whose barrier and microbial balance are disrupted. Treating the flakes without addressing the cause is why the cycle keeps repeating.

What’s happening

Dandruff and seborrheic dermatitis sit on the same spectrum. Both involve an oversensitivity to Malassezia, a yeast that lives naturally on every scalp and feeds on scalp oils. In susceptible individuals, its byproducts irritate the scalp and accelerate skin cell turnover. Skin cells that should mature and shed invisibly over weeks are pushed to the surface in days, arriving as visible clumps: flakes. Review: Confirm the Malassezia mechanism wording.

The problem is not the presence of the yeast, which is universal, but the scalp environment that allows it to flourish and the skin's reaction to it.

Sebum production, humidity, sweat, product buildup, stress, and an impaired scalp barrier all shape that environment. This is why results from anti-fungal shampoos are often temporary: they suppress the yeast without restoring the conditions of a healthy scalp. A chronically inflamed, flaking scalp is also a poor foundation for hair growth, which is why persistent dandruff and gradual thinning so often appear together. Frequently asked questions
Why does my dandruff keep coming back after shampoo works at first?
Anti-dandruff shampoos suppress the yeast involved, but they do not restore the scalp barrier or correct the oil balance that lets the problem recur. When you stop, conditions are unchanged and the cycle resumes.
Is dandruff caused by a dry scalp?
Usually the opposite. Most persistent dandruff occurs on oilier scalps, because the yeast involved feeds on scalp oils. Treating it with heavy moisturising oils often makes it worse.
Can dandruff cause hair loss?
Chronic scalp inflammation can disturb the follicle environment and contribute to increased shedding over time. Resolving the scalp condition is foundational to any hair concern.
Should I wash my hair more often or less often?
It depends on your sebum levels and the condition's severity, which is exactly what assessment establishes. Both overwashing and underwashing can worsen the cycle.
Is it seborrheic dermatitis or psoriasis?
They can look similar but behave differently and are managed differently. Distribution, scale type, and inflammation pattern under proper examination usually distinguish them.
